Most business websites are brochures. They tell visitors who you are, what you offer, and how to get in touch — and then hand the actual work back to you and your inbox. For a lot of businesses, that's fine. For a growing number, it's the bottleneck.

A website that *works like software* does more than describe your business. It runs part of it — taking bookings, generating quotes, showing clients their status — while you sleep. Here's how to tell which one you need.

## What "works like software" means

The line is simple: a brochure site *informs*, a software-like site *does*. The moment your website can complete a task a customer or your team would otherwise do by hand, it stops being marketing and starts being infrastructure.

Common examples we build in:

- **Booking and scheduling** that syncs to your calendar and takes deposits.
- **Instant quoting** so visitors get a price without waiting for an email.
- **Client dashboards** where customers self-serve status, files, or history.
- **Internal tools** that replace the spreadsheet holding your operations together.

## Signs you've outgrown a brochure site

You don't need custom functionality on day one. But a few signals mean it's time:

1. The same request lands in your inbox every day and you answer it manually.
2. Customers ask "can I just do this online?" and the answer is no.
3. Your team copies the same data between your website and other tools.
4. Growth means *more admin*, not just more revenue.

> If your website makes work for you instead of doing work for you, it's underbuilt.

## It doesn't have to be a big rebuild

The best part: adding software-like features rarely means starting over. We can layer a booking flow, a quoting tool, or a dashboard onto a site that's already converting well — the same [conversion-focused foundation](/blog/homepage-conversion), now doing more of the job.

And it's not all-or-nothing. Some of it is genuinely better handled by existing tools — which is exactly the [custom vs off-the-shelf](/blog/custom-software-vs-off-the-shelf) decision worth getting right before you build.

## The payoff

A brochure site earns you a lead. A website that works like software earns you a lead *and* handles the next three steps — so you spend less time on admin and more on the work only you can do. We build both, tailored to how your business actually runs, and manage the whole thing end to end.
